Case study

TableFlow

AI teammates for data tasks

View project
RoleDesign & Engineering
DisciplineProduct Design · AI
PlatformWeb app
Year2024

Context

The challenge

TableFlow is aI teammates for data tasks. The brief was to take something genuinely complex and make it feel effortless — an experience that's clear to use, cohesive across every surface, and solid enough for the team to keep building on.

Role

What I owned

I led TableFlow end to end as a product designer and design engineer — owning the experience from research and strategy all the way through to the shipped, production-grade build.

Product strategyUX & user flowsUI designDesign systemPrototypingFrontend engineering

Process

From first sketch to shipped

01

Discover

I started with the problem and the people TableFlow is for — auditing the current experience, aligning with stakeholders on goals, and mapping the core jobs the product had to do well.

02

Define

From there I shaped the structure: information architecture, the key user flows, and a focused scope for v1 — so there was a clear, prioritized path to build against.

03

Design

I designed the experience end to end, from low-fidelity flows to polished UI, and built a reusable design system that kept TableFlow consistent and quick to extend. Prototypes made the interactions tangible before any code.

04

Build

As a design engineer I worked hand in hand with engineering — and built parts of the frontend myself — turning the designs into a responsive, production-grade interface with every detail intact.

05

Ship & iterate

We shipped, watched how people actually used it, and iterated — tightening flows, smoothing edge cases, and hardening the foundations for whatever came next.

Outcome

The result

TableFlow came together as a polished, production-grade web app — designed and engineered as one seamless whole, ready to grow with the team.
